A **Metal–Organic Framework (MOF)** is a class of crystalline materials made by linking **metal ions or metal clusters** with **organic molecules called ligands or linkers**.

Think of it as a **3D scaffold**:

* the **metal nodes** act like joints
* the **organic linkers** act like bridges
* together they form a **highly porous network**

What makes MOFs important is that they often have:

* **very large internal surface area**
* **tunable pore size and chemistry**
* **ordered, repeatable structures**

Because of that, MOFs are widely studied for:

* **gas storage** such as hydrogen or methane
* **carbon dioxide capture**
* **gas separation**
* **catalysis**
* **drug delivery**
* **sensing**
* **water purification**
